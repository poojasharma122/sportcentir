Platform Reviews

Yahoo Fantasy

Known for its strong mobile experience, Yahoo Fantasy offers excellent live scoring, deep NHL player data, and easy-to-use league management tools.

Pros: Great app, deep stat tracking, active community.

Cons: Ads in free version.

Ideal for: Players wanting a top-tier app with balanced features.

Verdict: The gold standard for fantasy hockey platforms.

ESPN Fantasy

A clear, intuitive layout makes ESPN Fantasy a great starting point for new managers, while still offering competitive tools for seasoned players.

Pros: Beginner-friendly, accurate live scoring, reliable mobile performance.

Cons: Slightly less flexible on custom scoring than some competitors.

Ideal for: New players and casual leagues.

Verdict: The perfect introduction to fantasy hockey.

Fantrax

The most flexible platform for custom leagues, Fantrax is a favourite for those who want to fine-tune every scoring rule and roster slot.

Pros: Unlimited customisation, supports dynasty and keeper formats, deep player pool.

Cons: Less intuitive design for beginners.

Ideal for: Commissioners and advanced players.

Verdict: The ultimate choice for customised fantasy hockey setups.

CBS Sports

A sleek, fast mobile app with excellent live scoring and league communication tools. Great for managers who prioritise a smooth in-game experience.

Pros: Strong mobile performance, integrated chat, premium content.

Cons: Most leagues require a paid subscription.

Ideal for: Mobile-first fantasy hockey fans.

Verdict: A premium choice for those willing to invest in the best app experience.

OfficePools

Focused on simple pool setups, OfficePools makes it easy for workplaces and friend groups to run casual competitions without overwhelming settings.

Pros: Easy to set up, clear scoring, designed for group play.

Cons: Limited advanced features, paid-only.

Ideal for: Office pools and casual group leagues.

Verdict: Best for fun, social fantasy hockey.
